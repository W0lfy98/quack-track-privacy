Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add a duck to my collection?

Tap the Add tab, then take a photo or choose one from your library. Quack Track automatically removes the background, and if the photo has location/date info attached, it fills those in — you can edit either before saving.

Why does my duck's background look off, or not removed at all?

Background removal uses on-device Neural Engine processing and needs a real iPhone or iPad — it's not available in some virtualized/simulator environments. On a real device, if a photo has a very busy or low-contrast background, removal may be less clean; the original photo is always kept as a fallback if processing fails.

What does "re-homing" a duck do?

If you give a duck away to another Jeep owner, tap "Re-homed" on that duck's detail screen. It moves to the "Spent time with" section instead of disappearing — your full history is preserved. Tap "Welcome Back" any time to move it back.

What determines a duck's rarity?

Each duck is randomly assigned a rarity tier (Common through Mythic) the moment you add it, independent of where or when you found it. It's permanent once rolled and never changes.

Can I use my own photo as a Dash Shelf background?

Yes — in the Dash Shelf background picker, choose "Add Your Dash" to capture a landscape photo with the in-app camera, then drag the on-screen guides until the sample ducks line up with your real dashboard.

The app isn't finding my location or address for a duck.

Check that Location Services are enabled for Quack Track in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services. Without it, you can still type in a location manually when adding or editing a duck.
